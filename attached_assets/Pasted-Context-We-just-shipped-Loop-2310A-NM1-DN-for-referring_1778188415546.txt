REAL-WORLD FAILURE FROM A CHAJINEL REFERRAL TODAY:

VA Form 10-7080 from SF VAMC for veteran Peter Coit Mandler shows:
  Referring Provider: JESSICA CAPISTRANO
  Referring Provider NPI: 662_1375949

That is not an NPI. "662" is the VA station number for SF VAMC. "1375949" is a
7-digit VistA NEW PERSON file IEN. The composite is a VA-internal identifier that
VistA/HSRM dropped into the NPI field on the form. validateNpiOrThrow rejects it
(correctly — it fails Luhn). Rachel cannot save the referring provider, the PA form
blocks her, the claim cannot ship. This is not a one-off; it is a recurring VA
data-quality pattern across all CCN referrals.

Per TriWest's Claims Basics QRG (May 2023): "FIELD 17, 17b – Name of Referring
Provider or Other Source and NPI ... since all referrals must be pre-approved by VA
and can be tracked in TriWest's system, this field is not required." So for VA
payers, Loop 2310A is situational, not required, when REF*9F (referral number) is
populated.
