## 6. PRIOR AUTH — TWO MODES

The Prior Auth page currently assumes providers 
always initiate the authorization request.
This is wrong for VA Community Care and some 
other government programs where authorization 
is issued proactively with the referral.

This is also wrong for many commercial payers 
where the PA request goes through the payer's 
own portal (Availity, NaviNet) and the provider 
just needs to record the result.

Redesign the Prior Auth page with two distinct modes:

MODE A — "Record Received Authorization"
Use when: auth number arrives with a referral or 
is issued proactively by the payer before any 
request is submitted through ClaimShield.
Applies to: VA Community Care, some Medicaid 
managed care plans, some commercial payers.

Fields:
- Patient (search/select)
- Payer (search/select from payers table)
- Authorization Number (free text)
- Authorized Service(s) — repeating rows:
  CPT/HCPCS Code | Description | 
  Authorized Units | Authorized Visits
- Authorization Start Date
- Authorization End Date
- Referring/Ordering Provider Name
- Referring/Ordering Provider NPI
- Notes (free text)
- Source (dropdown): 
  VA Referral | Payer Portal | Fax | Phone | 
  Electronic | Other

MODE B — "Track PA Request"
Use when: provider must submit a PA request and 
track its status through approval or denial.
Applies to: most commercial payers, Medicare 
Advantage, some Medicaid plans.

Fields (in addition to Mode A fields):
- Request Submitted Date
- Request Method: Portal | Fax | Phone | Electronic
- Clinical Justification Notes
- Supporting Documents (file upload, optional)
- Status: Pending | Approved | Denied | 
  Partially Approved | Under Review | Appealing
- Status Last Updated Date
- Denial Reason (if denied)
- Appeal Deadline (if denied — calculated as 
  denial date + payer-specific appeal window)
- Approved Units (may differ from requested)
- Approved Date Range

SHARED FUNCTIONALITY:
- PA records link to patient record and display 
  on patient detail page under a "Authorizations" 
  section
- PA auth number pre-fills into the claim wizard 
  when creating a claim for that patient + payer 
  combination
- PA expiration warning: show amber badge on 
  patient record when auth expires within 14 days
- PA exhaustion warning: show amber badge when 
  used visits/units reach 80% of authorized amount
- PA list page shows all active auths with 
  status, expiration, and utilization at a glance
